As time went on, I began learning to do more and more things. I strated off only being allowed to remove light switch plates and helping prepare rooms before painting. Now I prepare the job site, paint everything, and focus on small details that will make it stand out. With over 12 years of experience, I am a few tasks away from being able to find painting jobs on my own.

Once I began college courses, I decided to get a more managable job that was more flexible with my school schedule. I was recommended by a friend and was given a job at McDonalds. Before my first official week of starting work, I was called in by the store manager on Thanksgiving. Given the circumstances, I was eager to work and wanted to make a good impression so I went in. After that, the manager saw that I was a quick learner and was dependable, so by that time next month, I was clocking in more hours than most of the workers there. Four months later, I found out that same manager was going to be relocated to a different location. Before leaving however, he was recommending me to move up to a lower level management position while still not even having a full year of experience.
